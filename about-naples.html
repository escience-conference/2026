Where History Meets the Sea

Naples (Napoli), perched on Italy’s southwestern coast overlooking the glittering Gulf of Naples, is a city where vibrant street life and ancient heritage blend seamlessly. As one of Europe’s oldest continuously inhabited cities, Naples offers visitors a fascinating tapestry of culture, art, and gastronomy wrapped in a uniquely spirited atmosphere.
With roots stretching back to the Greeks in the 2nd millennium BCE, Naples later became a key city of the Roman Empire and — under successive rulers including the Bourbons — one of the most influential cultural hubs in the Mediterranean. Its historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, filled with narrow alleys, grand piazzas, and architectural wonders that tell the story of centuries of artistic evolution.

Dive into Naples’ Cultural Treasures

National Archaeological Museum (Museo Archeologico Nazionale)

A cornerstone of European archaeology, this world-renowned museum houses one of the most important collections of ancient art and artifacts anywhere. Its halls showcase masterpieces from Pompeii and Herculaneum, Greek and Roman sculpture, frescoes, mosaics, and even extensive Egyptian antiquities, offering a deep dive into Mediterranean civilizations.


Sansevero Chapel (Cappella Sansevero)

Hidden in the heart of the historic center, this stunning Baroque chapel is famed worldwide for its breathtaking sculptures, most notably the Veiled Christ, admired for its astonishing marble work and emotional depth. The chapel’s blend of artistic genius and enigmatic symbolism makes it one of Naples’ unmissable cultural gems.


Bourbon Tunnel (Galleria Borbonica)

For a different slice of history, explore the Bourbon Tunnel — an atmospheric underground passage originally begun in the mid-19th century as a secret military escape route. Over the decades it has served many lives: from unfinished royal project to World War II air-raid shelter, and today is open for guided tours that reveal Naples’ subterranean stories.


Experiences that Capture the Spirit of Naples

Pizza by the Sea

No visit to Naples is complete without tasting its world-famous pizza. Legend has it that the iconic Neapolitan pizza was born here, with its simple yet sublime combination of dough, San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and basil. For a quintessential experience, enjoy a classic pizza at a seaside pizzeria along the Lungomare — where sea breezes and Gulf views elevate every bite.


Strolls Along the Waterfront

The scenic Lungomare Caracciolo and nearby Castel dell’Ovo provide perfect backdrops for a leisurely walk beside the sparkling Bay of Naples. Cafés and gelaterie line the promenade, inviting you to linger and soak in sunset vistas.


Historic Streets and Hidden Corners

Beyond its major sights, Naples unfolds through its vibrant streets. Wander ancient lanes filled with artisan workshops, traditional markets, and legendary landmarks like the Teatro San Carlo, Piazza del Plebiscito, and Spaccanapoli — a narrow roadway that cuts through the city’s historic soul.



Why Visit Naples

Naples is more than just a destination — it’s an experience: rich in history, alive with contemporary energy, and framed by breathtaking coastal beauty. Whether you’re drawn to world-class museums, dramatic underground explorations, or simply savouring pizza with a view of Vesuvius, Naples invites every visitor to discover its profound and lively spirit.
